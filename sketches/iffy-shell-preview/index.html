Past Lives

82

Nora and Hae Sung, two deeply connected childhood friends, are forced apart after Nora's family emigrates from South Korea. Twenty years later, they are reunited in New York for one fateful week that will determine the course of their lives.

Nora Young (born Na Young) is a playwright on the verge of her biggest breakthrough. Her marriage to Arthur, a fellow writer, is loving but has settled into the quiet rhythm of companionship rather than passion. When Hae Sung — her childhood friend and first love — contacts her through a mutual acquaintance, she agrees to meet him during his first visit to New York.

What begins as a polite reunion between old friends quickly deepens into something more. Over the course of seven days, Nora finds herself torn between the life she has built and the life she left behind. Hae Sung, still single, still carrying the memory of their childhood connection, represents a path not taken — a version of herself that might have been.

The story unfolds through a series of encounters — a coffee shop in Koreatown, a walk across the Brooklyn Bridge, a late-night conversation in Nora's apartment while Arthur is away at a conference. Each meeting peels back another layer of their shared history and unexplored feelings.

By the end of the week, Nora must make a choice — not between two men, but between two versions of herself. The decision she makes will define not just her relationship with Hae Sung, but her understanding of who she is and who she wants to become.
