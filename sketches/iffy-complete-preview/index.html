Past Lives

82

Nora and Hae Sung, two deeply connected childhood friends, are forced apart after Nora's family emigrates from South Korea. Twenty years later, they are reunited in New York for one fateful week.

Nora Young (born Na Young) is a playwright on the verge of her biggest breakthrough. Her marriage to Arthur, a fellow writer, is loving but has settled into the quiet rhythm of companionship rather than passion. When Hae Sung — her childhood friend and first love — contacts her through a mutual acquaintance, she agrees to meet him during his first visit to New York.

What begins as a polite reunion between old friends quickly deepens into something more. Over the course of seven days, Nora finds herself torn between the life she has built and the life she left behind. Hae Sung, still single, still carrying the memory of their childhood connection, represents a path not taken.

The story unfolds through a series of encounters — a coffee shop in Koreatown, a walk across the Brooklyn Bridge, a late-night conversation in Nora's apartment while Arthur is away. Each meeting peels back another layer of their shared history and unexplored feelings.
